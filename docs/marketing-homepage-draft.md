## Section 1: A relay you can actually own

Traditional Nostr relays usually live on someone else's server. Hyperpipe changes that. It lets you create and run relays from your own device, so the relay itself becomes something personal, portable, and easy to shape around a specific purpose.

Instead of relying on one general-purpose hosted relay, you can create a relay for your family, a project, a small community, a private file-sharing space, or a temporary coordination network that only needs to exist for a short time.

## Section 2: Why Hyperpipe is different

### Card 1

#### Title

Run a relay, not a server

#### Body

Hyperpipe removes most of the infrastructure burden that usually comes with relay operation. Creating a relay should feel closer to creating a room than provisioning a service.

### Card 2

#### Title

Your relay, your rules

#### Body

When the relay runs on your device, you are not depending on a third-party operator to keep your space alive. Your data stays local by default, and the relay's purpose and access model are yours to define.

### Card 3

#### Title

Private by design, not as an afterthought

#### Body

Hyperpipe is built for private and purpose-specific networks as much as public ones. You can create invite-only spaces, smaller trusted networks, and relays tailored to one group or one workflow.

### Card 4

#### Title

Local-first, but not isolated

#### Body

Trusted-peer sync and optional gateway reachability help make self-run relays more practical. You keep ownership without giving up usability.

## Section 4: How it works

### Step 1

Create a relay on your device.

Choose whether it should be private or public, open or invite-based, and whether it should be direct-only or remotely reachable.

### Step 2

Share it with the right people.

Use Nostr identity, discovery, and invite flows to make the relay discoverable, private, or tightly scoped to a trusted group.

### Step 3

Use it as a dedicated space.

Run it as a chat relay, a project relay, a file-sharing relay, or the foundation for a custom workflow or app.

## Section 5: More than a traditional relay

Hyperpipe is not only about making relay hosting easier. It opens up a broader possibility space for Nostr itself.

When relays are easy to create, cheap to run, and local-first by design, they stop looking like fixed public infrastructure and start looking like flexible network building blocks. That makes it practical to build smaller, more intentional spaces for speech, coordination, and collaboration.

And because Hyperpipe keeps Nostr identity and discovery in the loop, it extends the existing ecosystem instead of replacing it with a separate silo.
