# Hyperpipe Welcome Page Draft

This draft is written for a first-run or onboarding welcome page. It assumes the goal is to make the product immediately understandable and guide the user toward creating a first relay.

## Screen 1: Welcome

### Headline

Welcome to Hyperpipe.

### Subhead

This device can host relays.

### Body

Hyperpipe lets you create and run your own Nostr relays directly from your device. A relay can be private or public, invite-only or open, and shaped around one person, one group, one project, or one specific job.

### Primary CTA

Create my first relay

### Secondary CTA

How Hyperpipe works

### Small supporting line

No hosted server required for the core experience.

## Screen 2: Quick orientation

### Headline

What is a relay in Hyperpipe?

### Body

Think of it as a shared space you control.

You can create one for yourself, for a family or group, for a project, for a private chat, or for a temporary coordination network that only needs to exist for one purpose.

### Three bullets

- It runs on your device
- Your data stays local by default
- You decide who can join and how

### CTA

Continue

## Screen 3: Pick a simple starting point

### Headline

Start with a relay that fits a real need.

### Intro line

Most people understand Hyperpipe fastest when they create a small, concrete relay first.

### Recommended option

#### Title

Private relay

#### Body

A good starting point for personal use, family use, or a small trusted group.

### Additional option

#### Title

Public relay

#### Body

A good fit for a community, open collaboration space, or a relay others should be able to discover more easily.

### Additional option

#### Title

Direct-join-only relay

#### Body

Keep the relay off the public gateway network and use it only with the people you share it with directly.

### CTA

Choose relay settings

## Screen 4: Access model

### Headline

Choose how people join.

### Option 1

#### Title

Invite-only

#### Body

Best for private groups, family spaces, project coordination, and smaller trusted networks.

### Option 2

#### Title

Open

#### Body

Best for communities or public-facing spaces where easy participation matters more than tight access control.

### Helper line

You can create more than one relay, so you do not need one model to fit every use case.

### CTA

Create relay

## Screen 5: Success

### Headline

Your relay is ready.

### Body

You now have a shared space you control. From here, you can invite people, keep it private, make it discoverable, or use it as the starting point for files, chat, or a custom workflow.

### Primary CTA

Open my relay

### Secondary CTA

Create another relay

### Small supporting line

Hyperpipe works best when you create relays around specific people, projects, or purposes.
